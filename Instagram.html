      Profile Images

      Instagram does not want us to be able to isolate the profile image, here are steps to solve that.

      Method 1:

      1. Right click on the page and choose “view page source”
      2. Ctrl-F and search for og:image
      3. The long link in the code is a very small resolution profile image
      4. Copy the number before .jpg
      5. Ctrl-F again and search for that number (something like 183746353)
      6. The third instance of that number is a link to a higher resolution profile image

      In URL replace every \u0026 with &

      1. Search page source of images posts for 1080

      In URL replace every \u0026 with &

       

      Method 2:

      1. In chrome right-click to the side of the profile photo (not on it) and select inspect to open the developer panel
      2. In the dev panel select the sources tab to open a file tree for the page
      3. Expand any entries starting with “scontent”
      4. Image folders are nested here and grouped by resolution and typically ordered with higher resolution files at the top of the list
      5. Clicking on the file will open a preview in the Sources panel
      6. Right clicking the file name will allow you to open it in a new tab or in the network tab of the developer panel

      User numbers on IG are not particularly useful but you may want them for your report or legal requests. We can use the page source to find that information as well.

      • Right click on the page and choose “view page source”
      • Ctrl-F and search for “owner”:{“id”
      • The number at that sting of character is the user number, similar to 2673300545

      Followers

      You must be logged in to view the followers of a specific account. Manual capture of followers and following:

      • While logged in click on the following or followers link
      • Scroll to the bottom and then hit Ctrl-A which will select all entries
      • Right click and select “Copy selected links” (this works best in Firefox)
      • Paste into your OneNote or a spread sheet 

      Third party follower tool of choice:

      https://followerwonk.com | Follower analysis

       

      Complete Post Analysis

      1. First screen capture the page using Fireshot or your screen capture of choice
      2. Next save the image:
      3. Right click on the photo and choose “inspect” which will open the developer panel
      4. Method 1: Select the Network tab
        • click on the Img filter and then you may need to refresh the page (F5 key)
        • a list of images will appear
        • right-click on each and open it in a new tab to view
        • right-click on the image and choose “save as”
      5. Method 2: Select the Sources tab
        • Expand the entry that starts with “scontent”, this will reveal nested folders containing the images from the page
        • Once you find the one you want, right click on it and either open it in a new tab or hit save
      6. Paste the URL into a browser and download the full version of the suspect image.
      7. Scroll through the comments, and expand any if necessary, by clicking "+".
      8. Click the summary of likes below the heart icon.
      9. Scroll through them until all are loaded, select all with ctrl-a (command-a on Mac) 
      10. Paste these into a spreadsheet or your OneNote using ctrl-v
      11. Repeat with any other posts that you need in full
      12. Remember that you can use a script such as Instaloader to download all the photos, but it will only get the photos, not the comments…thus instructions for manual capture

       

      Download IG Videos (Stories)

    1. Open the video
    2. Right click next to the video (not on it) and select inspect
    3. Click on the network tab
    4. In the filter field type in MP4, make sure the “All” filter is also selected
    5. Refresh the video by playing it again or hitting F5
    6. In the dev panel the links to the video will show up, right-click and open in a new tab
    7. The video will load in a new tab, right-click and select save video as
